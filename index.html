About Me

Hey there! My name is Felix Su. I am a 2nd year intended Computer Science major at the University of California at Berkeley.

I started coding during my senior year in high school and haven't looked back since. Over the past two years I've developed a wider knowledge of different languages and data structures and am currently working to become a better full stack developer. I soon hope to be completely comfortable with the many ways to manage front and back end development.

If I'm not coding you can probably find me on the basketball court or going out to the tennis court for a set or two. I've been honing my basketball skills as much as possible this summer by heading out to the blacktop for 2-3 hours everyday. I've found that playing pick up games and shooting around with strangers is a great way to make friends. As one of my more casual hobbies I also record songs for my Soundcloud using Garageband.

I'm currently a Software Engineer at a start up in San Francisco called Beeyond Inc.

Long-term Projects

Ever since I arrived in the west coast, I've been excited by the startup culture and the style of product development teams in the Bay Area. I love the freedom, lack of conformity, and, most of all, the idea of creating something innovative that is unique and useful for many people. With that in mind, I started noting every large problem I encountered and developed possible solutions for them on paper.

However, after the initial rush of "I want to build the next Facebook" passed I've come to realize it is very important to start small and let the product build itself. I can't tell you how many "exciting ideas" I've had that I ended up trashing the very next week. My philosophy is to start with a simple problem, develop a solution, and let it sit. If that problem spontaneously pops up again and you find yourself saying "well, that solution would be pretty useful right now", then get to work. You might be on to something.

I've recently come across an idea that has been stuck in my head for a while now. So, I'm trying to learn best-practices and skills for building a public product as quickly as I can in order to get started. My goal is to build the framework of the product with a tech team and then develop strategies for breaking into the market and scaling the product properly. If you're interested in joining me or learning more, please contact me. I love meeting new people!
